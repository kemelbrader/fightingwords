Before the first shots were fired at the battles of Lexington and Concord, American colonists and British imperialists had already begun to fight on the ideological front. American and British pamphleteers struggled to determine the meaning of the revolution and the consequences of victory or defeat. Ultimately, their words would define what it meant to be an American. Fighting Words Online features videos of 41 dramatic readings, texts, and images from the original pamphlets from the University of Utah's rare book collections.
